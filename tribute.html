A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

11th President of India

Here's a time line of Abdul Kalam's life

Childhood and Education (1931-1955)
  • Early Life and Family Background: Abdul Kalam was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India to a humble family of boat owners.
  • Education and Early Career: He earned degrees in physics and aerospace engineering and began his career at the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO).
  • Contributions to Aeronautical Science and Defense Research: Kalam worked on various defense projects, including developing India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle.
Emergence as a Scientist (1955-1980)
  • Career at the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO): He rose to prominence as a scientist and played a key role in India's missile development program.
    Contributions to the Indian Missile Program: Kalam played a crucial role in the development of India's Agni and Prithvi missiles, which boosted India's defense capabilities.
    Achievements in Rocket Science and Aerospace Engineering: He made significant contributions to the fields of rocket science and aerospace engineering, earning him several prestigious awards and recognition.
Role in Indian Science and Politics (1980-2002)
  • Appointment as the Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister of India: In 1982, Kalam was appointed as the Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister of India and continued to play a key role in India's missile program.

    Contributions to the Development of India's Space Program: Kalam played a significant role in the development of India's space program and helped establish the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).

    Vision for India's Socio-Economic Development and Youth Empowerment: Kalam was a strong advocate for youth empowerment and education and wrote several books on these topics.

Presidency (2002-2007)
  • Election as the President of India in 2002: Kalam was elected as the President of India in 2002, becoming the first scientist to hold the position.

    Contributions to National Unity, Education and Technology: During his presidency, Kalam focused on promoting education and technology, and emphasized the importance of national unity.

    Continuing Legacy as the "People's President" and "Missile Man of India": After his presidency, Kalam continued to inspire millions with his vision for India's development and youth empowerment.

Post-Presidency and Death (2007-2015)
  • Continuing Work in Education and Youth Empowerment: Kalam continued to work towards promoting education and youth empowerment, delivering lectures and speeches across India.

    Passing away on July 27, 2015: Kalam passed away while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong, leaving behind a legacy as one of India's most visionary leaders.

    National Mourning and Legacy as a Visionary Leader and Inspiration to Millions: Kalam's death was mourned by the nation, and his legacy as a visionary leader and inspiration to millions continues to live on.
